    MFG chapter 5.1 Metric Spaces of Probability Measures

    1. Wasserstein Distances

    Let \(p \geqslant 1\). We denote by \(\mathcal{P}_p(E)\) the set of probability measures \(\mu\) on a metric space \((E,d)\) such that \(\int_E d(x_0,x)^p d\mu(x) < \infty\) for some (and hence any) \(x_0 \in E\).

    The \(p\)-Wasserstein distance between \(\mu, \nu \in \mathcal{P}_p(E)\) is then defined as

    \[ W_{p}(\mu, \nu)=\inf _{\pi \in \Pi(\mu, \nu)}\left[\int_{E \times E} d(x, y)^{p} d \pi(x, y)\right]^{1 / p} \]

    注意到 \(W_p(\mu,\nu)\) 除了依赖于概率测度 \(\mu\) 和 \(\nu\) 之外，还依赖于实际的距离函数 \(d\)。不同的 \(d\) 会导致 \(W_p(\mu,\nu)\) 的值不同，尽管 \(E\) 的拓扑结构和其上的概率测度空间没有发生变化。比方说，\(d\) 可以是以下形式：

    • \(d_{E \times E}\left(\left(x_{1}, x_{2}\right),\left(y_{1}, y_{2}\right)\right)=\left(d\left(x_{1}, y_{1}\right)^{q}+d\left(x_{2}, y_{2}\right)^{q}\right)^{1 / q},\quad \text{for some }q\in [1,\infty)\)
    • \(d_{E \times E}\left(\left(x_{1}, x_{2}\right),\left(y_{1}, y_{2}\right)\right)=\max \left(d\left(x_{1}, y_{1}\right), d\left(x_{2}, y_{2}\right)\right)\)

    \(\int_{E \times E} d(x, y)^{p} d \pi(x, y)\) 有时也被称作与 coupling \(\pi\) 相关的成本。

    记号 \(\Pi_{p}^{\mathrm{opt}}(\mu, \nu)\) 表示达到下界的 couplings \(\pi\) 的集合。

    \[ \Pi_{p}^{\mathrm{opt}}(\mu, \nu)=\left\{\pi \in \Pi_{p}(\mu, \nu):\left(W_{p}(\mu, \nu)\right)^{p}=\int_{\mathbb{R}^{d} \times \mathbb{R}^{d}} d(x, y)^{p} d \pi(x, y)\right\} \]

    此外，\(W_p(\mu,\nu)\) 可以验证满足以下距离的性质，因此可以被称作为一个距离：

    • 非负性: \(W_p(\mu,\nu) \geqslant 0\) and \(W_p(\mu,\nu) = 0 \Leftrightarrow \mu = \nu\)
    • 对称性: \(W_p(\mu,\nu) = W_p(\mu,\nu)\)
    • 三角不等式: \(W_p(\mu,\nu) \leqslant W_p(\mu,\lambda)+W_p(\lambda,\nu)\)

    Theorem 5.5 For any \(p \geqslant 1\) , if \(\left(\mu_{n}\right)_{n \geqslant 1}\) and \(\mu\) are in \(\mathcal{P}_{p}(E)\), \(\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} W_{p}\left(\mu_{n}, \mu\right)= 0\) if and only if \(\left(\mu_{n}\right)_{n \geqslant 1}\) converges toward \(\mu\) for the weak convergence of probability measures and:

    \[ \lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} \int_{E} d\left(x_{0}, x\right)^{p} d \mu_{n}(x)=\int_{E} d\left(x_{0}, x\right)^{p} d \mu(x) \]

    for one (and hence for all) \(x_{0} \in E\) . The latter is also equivalent to the fact that \(\left(\mu_{n}\right)_{n \geqslant 1}\) converges toward \(\mu\) for the weak convergence of probability measures and is \(p\) -uniformly integrable, namely

    \[ \lim _{r \rightarrow \infty} \sup _{n \geqslant 1} \int_{E} d\left(x_{0}, x\right)^{p} \mathbf{1}_{\left\{d\left(x_{0}, x\right) \geqslant r\right\}} d \mu_{n}(x)=0 \]
    Proof

    (\(\Rightarrow\)) assume \(\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} W_{p}\left(\mu_{n}, \mu\right)=0\)

    denote by \(\pi_n\) an element of \(\Pi_p^{opt}(\mu_n,\mu)\), for any \(n \geqslant 1\).

    For any bounded and uniformly continuous \(f:E\rightarrow \mathbb{R}\), we have

    \[ \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} &\quad\left|\int_{E} f(x) d \mu_{n}(x)-\int_{E} f(x) d \mu(x)\right| \\ &= \left|\int_{E \times E}(f(x)-f(y)) d \pi_{n}(x, y)\right|\\ &\leq \int_{E \times E} \left|f(x)-f(y)\right| d \pi_{n}(x, y)\\ &=\int_{\{(x, y) ; d(x, y)>\epsilon\}}\left|f(x)-f(y)\right| d \pi_{n}(x, y) + \int_{\{(x, y) ; d(x, y)\leq\epsilon\}}\left|f(x)-f(y)\right| d \pi_{n}(x, y)\\ &\leq 2M \int_{\{(x, y) ; d(x, y)>\epsilon\}} d \pi_{n}(x, y) + \epsilon \int_{\{(x, y) ; d(x, y)\leq\epsilon\}} d \pi_{n}(x, y)\\ &=2M\pi_n(\{d(x, y)>\epsilon\}) + \epsilon \pi_n(d(x,y)\leq \epsilon)\\ &\leq 2M\pi_n(\{d(x, y)>\epsilon\}) + \epsilon. \end{aligned} \end{equation*} \]

    Notice that

    \[ \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} 0\leftarrow W_p(\mu_n,\mu)&= \int_{E\times E} d(x, y)^{p} d \pi_n(x, y)\\ &= \int_{d(x,y)>\epsilon} d(x, y)^{p} d \pi_n(x, y)+\int_{d(x,y)\leq \epsilon} d(x, y)^{p} d \pi_n(x, y)\\ &> \epsilon^{p}\pi_n(d(x,y)>\epsilon) \end{aligned} \end{equation*} \]

    Let \(n \rightarrow \infty\), we have

    \[ \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \pi_n(d(x,y)>\epsilon) \rightarrow 0 \end{aligned} \end{equation*} \]

    Therefore, for any \(\epsilon >0\), we have

    \[ \limsup _{n \rightarrow \infty}\left|\int f d \mu_{n}-\int f d \mu\right| \leq \epsilon \]

    This implies:

    \[ \mu_{n} \xrightarrow{w} \mu \]

    Next, let us fix \(\epsilon > 0\).

    There exists a constant \(c_{\epsilon}>0\) such that for all \(a,b\geqslant 0\) we have \((a+b)^p \leqslant (1+\epsilon)a^p + c_{\epsilon}b^p\).

    So, for \(x,y \in E\), we have

    \[ d\left(x_{0}, x\right)^{p} \leqslant\left(d\left(x_{0}, y\right)+d(y, x)\right)^{p} \leqslant(1+\epsilon) d\left(x_{0}, y\right)^{p}+c_{\epsilon} d(y, x)^{p} \]

    and integrating both sides, we get

    \[ \int_{E\times E} d\left(x_{0}, x\right)^{p} d \pi_{n}(x,y) \leqslant(1+\epsilon) \int_{E\times E} d\left(x_{0}, y\right)^{p} d \pi_{n}(x,y)+c_{\epsilon} \int_{E \times E} d(y, x)^{p} d \pi_{n}(x, y) \]

    Notice that

    \[ \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} &\int_{E\times E} d\left(x_{0}, x\right)^{p} d \pi_{n}(x,y)=\int_{E} d\left(x_{0}, x\right)^{p} d \mu_{n}(x)\\ &\int_{E\times E} d\left(x_{0}, y\right)^{p} d \pi_{n}(x,y)=\int_{E}d\left(x_{0}, y\right)^{p} d \mu(y) \end{aligned} \end{equation*} \]

    and we have

    \[ \int_{E \times E} d(y, x)^{p} d \pi_{n}(x, y) = W_p(\mu_n,\mu)^p \rightarrow 0 \text{ as }n \rightarrow \infty \]

    This implies

    \[ \limsup _{n \rightarrow \infty} \int_{E} d\left(x_{0}, x\right)^{p} d \mu_{n}(x) \leqslant(1+\epsilon) \int_{E} d\left(x_{0}, y\right)^{p} d \mu(y). \]

    Take \(\epsilon \rightarrow 0\), by Skorohod's theorem + Fatou's lemma, we have

    \[ \limsup _{n \rightarrow \infty} \int_{E} d\left(x_{0}, x\right)^{p} d \mu_{n}(x) \leqslant \int_{E} d\left(x_{0}, y\right)^{p} d \mu(y) \leqslant \liminf _{n \rightarrow \infty} \int_{E} d\left(x_{0}, x\right)^{p} d \mu_{n}(x) \]
    \[ \Longrightarrow \lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} \int_{E} d\left(x_{0}, x\right)^{p} d \mu_{n}(x)=\int_{E} d\left(x_{0}, x\right)^{p} d \mu(x) \]

    (\(\Leftarrow\)) assume \(\mu_{n} \xrightarrow{w} \mu\) and \(\mathbb{E}_n[d(x_0,x)]\rightarrow \mathbb{E}[d(x_0,x)]\)

    Invoking Skorohod’s representation theorem, we can find a sequence of \(\mathbb{R}^d\)-valued random variables \((X_n)_{n\geqslant 1}\), constructed on some probability space \((\Omega,\mathcal{F},\mathbb{R})\) with \(\mathcal{L}(X_n) = \mu_n\) for any \(n > 1\) and converging almost surely to some random variable \(X\) with \(\mathcal{L}(X) = \mu\).

    Obviously \(W_{p}\left(\mu_{n}, \mu\right)^{p} \leqslant \mathbb{E}\left[d\left(X_{n}, X\right)^{p}\right]\), for any \(n\geqslant 1\).

    Therefore, it suffices to prove that the sequence \((X_n)_{n\geqslant 1}\) is \(p\)-uniformly integrable.

    For any \(r > 0\), we have

    \[ d^{p}=(d \wedge r)^{p}+d^{p} \cdot \mathbf{1}_{\{d \geq r\}}-(d \wedge r)^{p} \cdot \mathbf{1}_{\{d \geq r\}}. \]

    So

    \[ \mathbb{E}\left[d\left(x_{0}, X_{n}\right)^{p} \mathbf{1}_{\left\{d\left(x_{0}, X_{n}\right) \geqslant r\right\}}\right]=\mathbb{E}\left[d\left(x_{0}, X_{n}\right)^{p}\right]-\mathbb{E}\left[\left(d\left(x_{0}, X_{n}\right) \wedge r\right)^{p}\right]+r^{p} \mathbb{P}\left[d\left(x_{0}, X_{n}\right) \geqslant r\right] . \]

    By Portemanteau theorem, we have

    \[ \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} &\limsup _{n \rightarrow \infty} \mathbb{E}\left[d\left(x_{0}, X_{n}\right)^{p} \mathbf{1}_{\left\{d\left(x_{0}, X_{n}\right) \geqslant r\right\}}\right]\\ =&\limsup _{n \rightarrow \infty} \left\{ \mathbb{E}\left[d\left(x_{0}, X_{n}\right)^{p}\right]-\mathbb{E}\left[\left(d\left(x_{0}, X_{n}\right) \wedge r\right)^{p}\right]+r^{p} \mathbb{P}\left[d\left(x_{0}, X_{n}\right) \geqslant r\right]\right\}\\ \leqslant& \mathbb{E}\left[d\left(x_{0}, X\right)^{p}\right]-\mathbb{E}\left[\left(d\left(x_{0}, X\right) \wedge r\right)^{p}\right]+r^{p} \mathbb{P}\left[d\left(x_{0}, X\right) \geqslant r\right] \\ =&\mathbb{E}\left[d\left(x_{0}, X\right)^{p} \mathbf{1}_{\left\{d\left(x_{0}, X\right) \geqslant r\right\}}\right]. \end{aligned} \end{equation*} \]

    Letting \( r \to \infty \), and using the fact that \( X \in L^p \), we conclude that the sequence \( (X_n)_n \) is uniformly \( p \)-integrable.

    Combining \(X_n \xrightarrow{a.s} X\), we can exchange the limit

    \[ \lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} W_{p}\left(\mu_{n}, \mu\right)\leqslant\lim_{n\rightarrow \infty}\mathbb{E}[d(X_n,X)^p]=\mathbb{E}[\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}d(X_n,X)^p] = 0. \]

    Skorohod's Representation Theorem

    Let \(\mu_{n}\), \(n \in N\) be a sequence of probability measures on a metric space \(S\).
    suppose that \(\mu_{n} \xrightarrow{w} \mu\) on \(S\).
    Suppose also that the support of \(\mu\) is separable.
    Then there exist random variables \(X_{n}, X\) defined on a common probability space \((\Omega, F, P)\) such that \(\mathcal{L}(X_n) = \mu_n\) and \(\mathcal{L}(X)=\mu\), \(X_{n} \xrightarrow{\text { as. }} X\)

    Skorohod's theorem + Fatou's lemma

    \[ \begin{aligned} \int_{E} d\left(x_{0}, x\right)^{p} d \mu(x) & =\mathbb{E}\left[d\left(x_{0}, X\right)^{p}\right] \\ & = \mathbb{E}\left[\lim_{n \rightarrow \infty}d\left(x_{0}, X_n\right)^{p}\right]\\ & \leqslant \liminf _{n \rightarrow \infty} \mathbb{E}\left[d\left(x_{0}, X_{n}\right)^{p}\right] \\ & \leqslant \liminf _{n \rightarrow \infty} \int_{E} d\left(x_{0}, x\right)^{p} d \mu_{n}(x) \end{aligned} \]

    Portemanteau Theorem

    Let \(E\) be a metric space, and let \(\mu_n, \mu \in \mathcal{P}(E)\) be probability measures. We say that

    \(\mu_n \xrightarrow{w} \mu\) if any of the following equivalent conditions is true:

    • (Definition of weak convergence)
      For every bounded continuous function \(f : E \to \mathbb{R}\), \(\int f \, d\mu_n \to \int f \, d\mu.\)

    • (Upper bound on closed sets)
      For every closed set \(F \subset E\), \(\limsup_{n \to \infty} \mu_n(F) \leq \mu(F).\)

    • (Lower bound on open sets)
      For every open set \(G \subset E\), \(\liminf_{n \to \infty} \mu_n(G) \geq \mu(G).\)

    • (Convergence on continuity sets)
      For every set \(A\) such that \(\mu(\partial A) = 0\) (i.e., \(\mu\)-continuity set), \(\mu_n(A) \to \mu(A).\)

    • (Upper bound for upper semicontinuous functions)
      For every bounded upper semicontinuous function \(f : E \to \mathbb{R}\), \(\limsup_{n \to \infty} \int f \, d\mu_n \leq \int f \, d\mu.\)

    • (Lower bound for lower semicontinuous functions)
      For every bounded lower semicontinuous function \(f : E \to \mathbb{R}\), \(\liminf_{n \to \infty} \int f \, d\mu_n \geq \int f \, d\mu.\)

    These conditions are all equivalent and characterize weak convergence of probability measures on metric spaces.
